B.Sc. (Hons.) Forestry

Duration: 4 Years

B.Sc. (Hons.) Forestry curriculum contains an integrated forestry syllabus in accordance with ICAR guidelines. The 1st & 2nd semesters comprise of basic and fundamental courses, the 3rd & 4th semesters focus on principles, 5th & 6th semester deal with production systems, 7th and 8th semester aim at Skill and Entrepreneurship Development.

B.Sc. (Hons.) Forestry, includes an innovative component called student READY (“Rural Entrepreneurship Awareness Development Yojana”) programme, an essential prerequisite for the award of degree to ensure hands on experience and practical training to students. The programme aims to reorient graduates of Forestry for ensuring and assuring employability and develop entrepreneurs for emerging knowledge in the field of Forestry.

Project Work & Dissertation

10 weeks (0+10 credit) to be taken up during the VIII semester.
  • This course shall provide the B.Sc. (Hons.) Forestry students a practical approach towards the design of research experiments (both lab-based and field-based), analysis and interpretation of data and technical writing skills.
  • Each student shall work on a specific research project to be identified with the help of the supervising teacher.
  • They shall also prepare and present a proposed plan of work (PPW) specifying the objectives and procedures of the study and present the same before an audience consisting of faculty and students.
  • The research work will be conducted leading to the preparation of a project report in the format and style of M.Sc. thesis.

Experiential Learning

It helps the student in developing competence and capability to acquire skills, expertise, and confidence to start their own enterprise and to become job creators instead of job seekers. Any one of the modules (0+10 credits) to be taken up during VIII semester:
  • Production and Marketing of high value forest produce
  • Raising Quality Planting Materials for forest regeneration
  • Apiculture/Sericulture/Mushroom cultivation
  • Ecotourism
  • Wild Animal Health Management

Student Ready Programme

The term READY refers to “Rural Entrepreneurship Awareness Development Yojana”. Important components of Student READY programme are:
  • Forestry Work Experience (FoWE)
  • Experiential Learning (EL)
  • Project Work & Dissertation

Forestry Work Experience (FoWE)

This is a programme designed to provide the students a first hand learning experience on various aspects of forest range management, administration, ecosystem structure and functioning, wherein the students closely interact with forest officials and also to accompany them on routine missions such as raids, patrols, perambulation, executing warrants, serving summons, apprehending poachers and other offenders etc. During this time, they are also actively engaged in various other forestry operations and industrial exposure. The students spend about 24 weeks during the VII semester with (0+20) credit hours. The program envisages:
  • Orientation 
  • Watershed Village attachment
  • Urban Forestry attachment
  • Forest Range attachment
  • Wildlife attachment
  • Industrial attachment
